# Mission/Vision:

*Making a bet on Arizona.*

The **1912 Institute** is a nonpartisan research institution dedicated to making Arizona the best place in the world to pursue a high-quality education, raise a family, and build a business and career. Our history, culture, natural resources, and capacity for growth uniquely position our state to be a national model in how dynamism, economic independence, and effective government that empowers everyday people can work in tandem to improve lives and livelihoods.

**We're making a bet on Arizona,** challenging people to think boldly about what our state is capable of in the 21st century.

## Why Arizona?

Our state was founded by intrepid souls who braved summer heat and grueling droughts to make a home for themselves among bramblebush and rolling sandstorms. Arizonans are at their best in the face of seemingly insurmountable obstacles, motivated by the thrill of doing tough things. Our ancestors’ spirit of daring still satiates the Grand Canyon State — a beacon of hope in a nation divided and distressed. The 1912 Institute was founded to tell this story and to shape it for generations to come.

## The Desert Way

Our model is focused on impact and grounded in Arizona’s unique . We call it the “Desert Way.”

We inform policymakers, lobbyists, industry leaders, and everyday citizens about policies that will prepare our state for shared and sustained growth. We are in this for the long term, building up institutional knowledge in government and industry so that our future leaders will be prepared to confront the grand challenges we will face in the 21st century and beyond. We accomplish this mission by:

Publishing and distributing op-eds, research papers, and policy briefs on issues facing Arizonans.
Conducting thorough research on the inner workings of state and local governments, businesses, and other adjacent institutions who serve the people of Arizona.
Grounding all research in a deep understanding of Arizona, Western, and American history.
Hosting events with prominent state leaders and everyday citizens to illuminate grand challenges facing our state, and potential solutions.
Maintaining a community of business leaders, local and statewide officials, scholars, and subject-matter experts dedicated to making Arizona the greatest state in the Union.
Distributing civic educational materials to schools and other educational institutions so that Arizona children and adults can gain mastery over their state and nation’s history.
